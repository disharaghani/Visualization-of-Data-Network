Visualization of Computer Networks

Gist of Project:

    Analysis of networks is important for operation, research and development of networks and design of new protocols. For these reasons we have developed a visualization tool to make analysis faster and easier. Visualization of computer networks is viewed as a huge potential catalyst for implementing our algorithm in an effective way.

Ford-Fulkerson algorithm:By(L. R. Ford Jr. and D. R. Fulkerson)

1.Edmondsâ€“Karp algorithm:

    Ford-Fulkerson algorithm is a greedy approach for calculating the maximum possible flow in a network or a graph. A term, flow network, is used to describe a network of vertices and edges with a source (S) and a sink (T). Each vertex, except S and T, can receive and send an equal amount of stuff through it. S can only send and T can only receive stuff. We can visualize the understanding of the algorithm using a flow of liquid inside a network of pipes of different capacities.

2.Capacity Scaling algorithm:

    The idea behind capacity scaling is to augment the highest flow paths first to reach our maximum flow faster. We do this by limiting our scope to a D-residual graph, i.e. a residual graph with all edges with < D capacity removed.

Applications:

    1.Water distribution pipeline
    2.Bipartite matching problem
    3.Circulation with demands

Network Graph:

Terminologies Used:

Source:

A vertex has all outward edge and no inward edge.

Sink:

Sink will have all inward edge,no outward edge.

Max-Flow

It is defined as the maximum amount of flow that the network would allow to flow from source to sink.

Augmenting Path:

It is the path available in a flow network.

Residual Graph:

It represents the flow network that has additional possible flow.

Residual Capacity:

It is the capacity of the edge after subtracting the flow from the maximum capacity.

Bottle Neck capacity:

Minimum capacity of any edge on the path.
